 82      '''Loosely represents a SAGA job as defined in GFD.90 
 83       
 84      A 'Job' represents a running application instance, which may consist of one 
 85      or more processes.  Jobs are created by submitting a Job description to 
 86      a Job submission system -- usually a queuing system, or some other service 
 87      which spawns jobs on the user's behalf. 
 88   
 89      Jobs have a unique ID (see get_job_id()), and are stateful entities -- their 
 90      'state' attribute changes according to a well defined state model: 
 91   
 92      A job as returned by job.Service.create(jd) is in 'New' state -- it is not 
 93      yet submitted to the job submission backend.  Once it was submitted, via 
 94      run(), it will enter the 'Pending' state, where it waits to get actually 
 95      executed by the backend (e.g. waiting in a queue etc).  Once the job is 
 96      actually executed, it enters the 'Running' state -- only in that state is 
 97      the job actually consuming resources (CPU, memory, ...). 
 98   
 99      Jobs can leave the 'Running' state in three different ways: they finish 
100      successfully on their own ('Done'), they finish unsuccessfully on their own, 
101      or get canceled by the job management backend ('Failed'), or they get 
102      actively canceled by the user or the application ('Canaceled'). 
103   
104      The methods defined on the Job object serve two purposes: inspecting the 
105      job's state, and initiating job state transitions. 
106      '''
